Introducing myself to Ping Pong

I am a beginner to this sport. When I moved to Missouri in April of 2016, I found a new love of mine: Ping Pong. It was nested in the basement of my girlfriend's parent's house, a ping pong table. It was inconspicuous, but seemed to call to me. I picked up the paddle and proceeded to badger my girlfriend for a quick rally (to hit the ball back and forth for fun). She agreed, and several minutes later I found myself in love.

Challenges, Joy, and Defeat

My first steps into Ping Pong were a strange mix of childlike excitement and the staggering realization that I was outclassed. My girlfriend and her brother were my main adversaries, and growing up with a ping pong table has had an obvious effect on them!

As a beginner, I faced many problems that forced me to adapt and overcome:

  • Getting a Grip:
    • Trying to figure out which hand to hold the paddle was surprisingly difficult. I began using my left hand, but ultimately switched to my right, and found that my control was much better.
  • Staying on the Table:
    • One of my biggest downfalls is position and power: the two P's that are fundamental to a good hit. Position determines where you aim the ball, and power determines how much force you put into the hit. In my case, many hits were either not making it past the net, or going completely off the table.
